Abstract

This paper investigates the role and effectiveness of fiscal policy in stimulating economic recovery in the aftermath of the COVID-19 pandemic. Drawing upon post-Keynesian and modern macroeconomic theory, we examine government spending, taxation, and transfer programs as tools to manage aggregate demand and mitigate recessionary effects. Through analysis of data from major economies between 2020 and 2024, the paper finds that expansive fiscal policy had short-term positive effects on GDP growth and employment, but also contributed to inflationary pressures and rising public debt.
